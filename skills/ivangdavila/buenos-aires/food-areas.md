# Buenos Aires: Best Areas for Dining

## Neighborhood Dining Guide

### Palermo Soho - Foodie Central

**Character:** Trendy, international, Instagram-worthy. The epicenter of BA's modern food scene.

**Best For:**
- Contemporary Argentine cuisine
- International variety
- Brunch culture
- Natural wine
- Date nights

| Category | Top Picks |
|----------|-----------|
| Modern Argentine | Anafe, Proper |
| Steak | Don Julio, La Carniceria |
| Brunch | Oui Oui, Ninina, Cuervo |
| Asian | Gran Dabbang, Osaka |
| Cocktails | Victoria Brown, Frank's |
| Coffee | LAB, Coffee Town |
| Ice Cream | Rapanui, Chungo |

**Price Level:** $$-$$$$
**Reservations:** Essential for popular spots on weekends

### Palermo Hollywood - Night Dining

**Character:** Later nights, more bars, media industry crowd.

**Best For:**
- Cocktail-paired dinners
- Late night eats
- Industry crowd
- Creative cocktails

| Category | Top Picks |
|----------|-----------|
| Cocktails | 878, Nicky NY |
| Late night | Burger Joint |
| Peruvian | La Mar |
| Mediterranean | Olsen |
| Tacos | La Fabrica del Taco |

**Price Level:** $$-$$$
**Scene:** Gets busy after 10pm

### Villa Crespo - The New Hot Spot

**Character:** Where the chefs went when Palermo got expensive. Serious food, less tourist.

**Best For:**
- Best steakhouses
- Chef-driven restaurants
- Value for quality
- Locals + in-the-know visitors

| Category | Top Picks |
|----------|-----------|
| Steak | Don Julio, Lo de Jesus |
| Modern | Anafe (technically Palermo border) |
| Craft Beer | Strange Brewing |
| Pasta | Fresh pasta shops everywhere |
| Bakery | Various panaderia artesanales |

**Price Level:** $$-$$$$
**Reservations:** Essential for Don Julio (weeks ahead)

- Classic Argentine
- Business lunches
- Hotel dining

**Best For:**

- Market eating

- Tango dinner shows

| Category | Top Picks |
|----------|-----------|
| Market | Mercado de San Telmo |

### Puerto Madero - Waterfront Dining

**Character:** Corporate, waterfront, modern. Expense account territory.

**Best For:**
- Business dinners
- Waterfront views
- Safety-conscious visitors
- Special occasions

| Category | Top Picks |
|----------|-----------|
| Fine Dining | Chila |

| Italian | La Parolaccia, Sottovoce |

## Food Markets

### Mercado de San Telmo

**What:** Historic covered market, increasingly foodie destination.
**When:** Daily, but Sunday is special (outdoor antique market adjacent)
**Must-try:**
- Coffee at Coffee Town stand
- Empanadas from various
- Wine tasting
- Oysters (when available)

### Mercado de Belgrano

**What:** Traditional neighborhood market
**When:** Daily
**Best for:** Fresh produce, Asian ingredients (near Chinatown)

### Feria de Mataderos

**What:** Traditional gaucho fair, folk culture, regional food
**When:** Sundays
**Getting there:** Far from center, taxi recommended
**Best for:** Locro, empanadas, authentic experience

## Dining Strategy by Day

### Monday
- Many restaurants closed or "descanso" (rest day)
- Chains and hotels reliable
- Good night for casual neighborhood spots

### Tuesday-Thursday
- Full operations
- Easier reservations than weekend
- Good for popular restaurants

### Friday
- Busy after work (Las Canitas, Palermo Hollywood)
- Reservations recommended
- Nightlife kicks off

### Saturday
- Peak dining day
- Reserve ahead for popular spots
- Late dinner (9:30-10pm start)
- After-dinner drinks culture

### Sunday
- **MANY RESTAURANTS CLOSED**
- Plan ahead
- Puerto Madero, Palermo Soho most reliable
- San Telmo market area busy
- Family lunch tradition
